Seeing networks in Wellington

MDDN 201 - Madisyn Muggeridge

Wellington infrastructure

Manhole Covers Various Antennas Street Markings Cameras Power lines and cables

Manhole Covers

Manhole covers are the access points to uncovering what lies underneath Wellington. They are easily accessible and provide entry to the networking infrastructure that hosts, stores, and processes information. Connecting us to numerous networks around the world.

Various Antennas

Various antennas are other types of networking infrastructure that can be found above ground. Antennas receive radio waves and create electrical signals that can be transferred into different systems. They can be found in different formats all over Wellington, as I have captured them found on cars, roofs, and at traffic lights.

Street Markings

Street markings are used to aid the maintenance and construction work that may need to take place surrounding underground infrastructure. Various symbols, letters, and lines are used to convey the location, configuration, type of service, and amount of underground services at given points.

Infrastructure of Wellington - Cameras

Wireless cameras operate through the transmission of the video captured through a radio transmitter to a receiver. This allows the video to be accessed over the network and onto your device. Cameras can be seen used everywhere, whether it be for monitoring traffic flow at traffic lights or for security at someone's home or business.

Power lines and cables

Powerlines are built up of cables that distribute electrical energy across large distances to connect networks and provide power to users across the different networks. Cables connect Wellington to networks across the country and the world.
